QueryComment is database observability built for engineering teams who are tired of finding out about slowdowns from users. Instead of static thresholds and single-metric dashboards, QueryComment watches your database across three correlated layers — query behavior, engine internals, and infrastructure — and tells you *why* performance is degrading, not just *that* it is.

## What Makes QueryComment Different

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Three-Layer Observability" icon="layer-group">
    QueryComment correlates query fingerprints, engine internals, and infrastructure signals simultaneously — so a single slow query tells the full story from SQL to disk.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Drift Detection" icon="chart-line">
    Statistical models baseline every query individually and flag regressions before they cross a static threshold. You get days of warning instead of a pager alert at 2 AM.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Root Cause Analysis" icon="magnifying-glass-chart">
    A causal reasoning engine connects anomalies across all three layers into a directed chain — so you see "autovacuum blocked → dead tuple bloat → buffer eviction → disk I/O saturation" as one finding, not four unrelated alerts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How Onboarding Works

You can go from zero to observability in three steps, with no code changes and no query proxying required.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect Your Database">
    Deploy the QueryComment agent as a sidecar container or standalone binary. Point it at your database and supply your API key. The agent reads from stats views — it never modifies your database.
  </Step>
  <Step title="QueryComment Baselines Your Queries">
    The agent immediately begins fingerprinting queries and building latency baselines using exponential weighted moving models. Within 15 minutes you'll see your first query performance data. Within a day, drift detection has enough history to flag regressions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get Alerted Before Users Notice">
    When a query's latency trend deviates from its seasonal baseline, QueryComment fires an alert with a causal explanation attached — not just "p99 latency is high," but the full chain of contributing factors traced across query, engine, and infrastructure layers.
  </Step>
</Steps>
